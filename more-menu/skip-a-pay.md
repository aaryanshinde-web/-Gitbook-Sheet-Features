# Skip-a-Pay

## Summary

Skip-a-Pay allows eligible members to defer a scheduled loan payment by one month — with the deferred amount added to the end of the loan term — without incurring a late fee or negative credit bureau reporting. For business members managing seasonal cash flow fluctuations or facing a temporary liquidity constraint, Skip-a-Pay provides a credit union-authorised mechanism to preserve operating cash in a given month without impacting the loan account standing or the member's credit profile.

## Key Use Cases

Business members with seasonal revenue cycles use Skip-a-Pay during a slow month to preserve operating cash for payroll and vendor obligations, deferring the loan payment to a period when cash flow has recovered. Members facing an unexpected one-time expense — equipment repair, emergency tax payment, or urgent vendor settlement — use Skip-a-Pay to free up the loan payment amount for the immediate obligation without defaulting on the loan. Retail members managing personal cash flow around irregular income schedules use Skip-a-Pay as an authorised breathing room mechanism, with the full transparency that the deferred payment extends the loan term by one month.

## Step-by-Step Guide

**Step 1 — Navigate to Skip-a-Pay from the More Menu**

From the More menu, select **Skip-a-Pay** to open the eligible loan list. The platform automatically identifies which loans are eligible for deferment based on the credit union's Skip-a-Pay program criteria — loans that do not qualify are excluded from the selection screen.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (47).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 2 — Select the Loan and Review the Deferment Terms**

Select the loan for which the next payment will be skipped. The screen displays the payment amount being deferred, the new loan payoff date after adding one month to the term, and any applicable Skip-a-Pay fee charged by the credit union. Review the terms carefully before proceeding.

<figure><img src="../.gitbook/assets/image (48).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 3 — Accept Terms and Confirm**

Check the acknowledgement checkbox confirming the deferment terms and click **Submit**. A confirmation screen confirms that the payment has been successfully deferred and provides the updated next payment due date. The loan account status remains current throughout the deferment period.
