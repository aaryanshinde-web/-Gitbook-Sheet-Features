# Travel Notice

_Module: Banking › More › Travel Notice_

## Summary

The Travel Notice feature lets you notify the credit union of upcoming travel so your cards continue to work while you are away. A travel notice temporarily enables international transactions for the selected cards at the selected destinations, reducing the risk of fraud-prevention blocks on legitimate travel purchases. You can view active itineraries and remove them once your trip is complete.

## At a Glance

| Attribute | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Module | Banking › More › Travel Notice |
| Who Can Use | All nFinia Digital Banking members with an active card |
| Scope | Per-card, per-destination, per-date-range |
| Effect | Enables international transactions during the travel window |
| Reversible | Yes — remove itinerary at any time |
| Availability | 24 / 7 — via web or mobile |

## Key Use Cases

| Use Case | Description |
| --- | --- |
| **International travel** | Enable card use in specific countries during a trip |
| **Domestic out-of-pattern travel** | Reduce fraud declines for unusual travel locations |
| **Multi-card trip** | Add a single travel notice that covers multiple cards |
| **Back-to-back trips** | Maintain multiple overlapping itineraries |

## Step-by-Step Guide

_Navigation: Banking › Cards › (or More menu) › Travel Notice_

### Step 1 — Open the Cards Dashboard

From the top navigation, click **Cards** to open the Cards dashboard. The right-side **Related Links** panel and the top **More** menu both expose the Travel Notice entry point. Click **More** and select **Travel Notice**, or use the Related Links shortcut.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Cards_List.png" alt="Cards dashboard with access to Travel Notice via More menu and Related Links" data-size="original"><figcaption><p>Step 1: From the Cards dashboard, open <strong>More</strong> and select <strong>Travel Notice</strong>.</p></figcaption></figure>

### Step 2 — Enter Travel Details

The Travel Notice form has two tabs: **Set Travel Notice** (to add a new notice) and **Travel Itineraries** (to view existing notices). On the Set Travel Notice tab, select the card(s) you will be traveling with, select the destination location(s) you will be visiting, and choose the travel date range (year and month). Click **Save Travel Notice** to submit.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Travel_Notice_Form.png" alt="Set Travel Notice form" data-size="original"><figcaption><p>Step 2: On the <strong>Set Travel Notice</strong> tab, select cards, locations, and dates, then click <strong>Save Travel Notice</strong>.</p></figcaption></figure>

### Step 3 — Review Travel Itinerary

A success banner appears: **"Your travel notice request has been created successfully."** Switch to the **Travel Itineraries** tab to see all active notices, including the card, destination(s), and date range. Click **Remove Itinerary** to cancel a notice or **Edit Itinerary** to update the dates or locations.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/Travel_Notice_Itinerary.png" alt="Travel Itineraries tab listing active notices" data-size="original"><figcaption><p>Step 3: Review active itineraries under the <strong>Travel Itineraries</strong> tab. Edit or remove a notice at any time.</p></figcaption></figure>

> **Note:** A travel notice does not override other card controls. If your card has location controls enabled, the destination must also be on the allowed list. Consider reviewing **Card Controls › Location Controls** before you travel.
