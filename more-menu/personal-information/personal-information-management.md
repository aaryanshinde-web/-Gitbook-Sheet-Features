# Personal Information Management

> **Module:** Banking › More → Personal Information |

## Summary

The Personal Information section allows members to view and update their contact details, mailing and residential addresses, and manage trusted devices registered to their account — all without visiting a branch or calling member services. Keeping personal information current is essential for ensuring that one-time passcodes reach the right phone number, alert notifications are delivered to an active email address, official correspondence arrives at the correct mailing address, and identity verification succeeds when performing sensitive account actions.

The module is organised into tabs: **Devices** (trusted devices registered to the account), **Contact Info** (phone numbers and email addresses), **Mailing Address**, and **Residential Address**. Changes to sensitive contact fields such as phone numbers and email addresses require OTP verification using the existing contact on file before the new information can be saved — this prevents unauthorised changes to contact details that are also used for security verification.

Members who manage their own personal information through digital banking can complete address changes, phone number updates, and email address changes entirely through self-service in a few minutes, eliminating a common reason for branch or phone-centre contact.

**At a Glance**

| Attribute | Detail |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Module | More > Personal Information |
| Sections | Devices, Contact Info, Mailing Address, Residential Address |
| Verification | OTP confirmation required for phone and email changes |
| Trusted Devices | View, review, and remove registered trusted devices |
| Address Types | Mailing (for statements and cards) and Residential (for identity verification) |

## Key Use Cases


Member with a new mobile number edit Contact Info, update the phone number, and verify the change via OTP, ensures one-time passcodes and SMS alerts are delivered to the member's current phone number.

Member with a new email address edit Contact Info, update the email, and verify the new address, keeps email-based OTP delivery and eDocument notifications functional and up to date.

Member who has moved edit Mailing Address with the new address details and save, ensures statements, cards, and official correspondence are sent to the correct location.

Member checking current records view the Mailing and Residential Address screens, allows members to quickly confirm what the credit union has on file without calling or visiting a branch.

## Step-by-Step Guide

\| _Navigation: Dashboard > More > 'Personal Information'._ |

**Step 1 — Start from Dashboard**

After logging in, you land on the Dashboard, which displays all account balances, upcoming payments, quick-action tiles, and the top navigation bar. Personal Information is accessible from the More menu in the navigation bar.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/img_eb0a7d21423c.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 2 — Open the More Menu**

Click **More** in the top navigation bar. The More options panel expands to show available features, including Personal Information. Select **Personal Information** to navigate to the personal information management page. You can also navigate directly from the Dashboard to Personal Information if that shortcut is configured.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (1) (1) (1) (1) (1) (1) (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 3 — Personal Information Page — Devices Tab**

The Personal Information page loads with the **Devices** tab selected by default. This tab displays a list of all trusted devices currently registered to your account, showing each device's name or identifier. A **Forget Device** button appears next to each entry, allowing you to remove a device from the trusted list immediately — useful if you have replaced a device or if you no longer want a particular device to have saved session access.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/img_d1c115b8a116.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 4 — View Contact Information**

Select the **Contact Info** tab to see your registered phone numbers and email addresses on file. The information is displayed in a form format with clearly labelled fields. To update a phone number or email address, click the edit option for that field, enter the new information, and complete the OTP verification step. The OTP will be sent to your current contact on file to confirm your identity before the update is applied.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/img_f99177713c01.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 5 — View Mailing Address**

Select the **Mailing Address** tab to view the address where the credit union sends your statements, debit and credit cards, and official correspondence. An **Edit addresses** button is available to update the mailing address if you have moved or need to direct mail to a different location.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/img_da4b7977eac6.png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 6 — View Residential Address**

Select the **Residential Address** tab to view the home address on file with the credit union, which is used for identity verification purposes. An **Edit addresses** button is available if the residential address needs to be updated.

<figure><img src="../../.gitbook/assets/image (2) (1) (1) (1) (1) (1).png" alt=""><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 7 — View Trusted Device Detail**

When a device has been successfully registered as trusted, the Trusted Device section displays a shield icon with a confirmation message indicating the current device is trusted. This status means the device is recognised for future logins and may bypass additional MFA prompts depending on your credit union's session security configuration.
