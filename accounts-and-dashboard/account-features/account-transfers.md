# Account Transfers

## Summary

Account Transfers enables members to move funds between their own accounts within Summerville Credit Union — between checking, savings, money market, and loan accounts — in real time. For business members managing liquidity across multiple accounts, this feature is the primary mechanism for funding a payment account before an ACH batch processes or sweeping excess operating funds into a higher-yield savings account at the end of the day.

## Key Use Cases

Business members use Account Transfers to fund a business checking account from a savings reserve before a scheduled payroll ACH runs, make a same-day loan payment to avoid a late fee, and reallocate operating cash between accounts based on daily cash flow requirements. Members with multiple accounts use transfers to maintain target balances across accounts — for example, keeping a minimum balance in savings while directing surplus to checking for operational use. The immediate settlement of internal transfers makes this the preferred mechanism when timing is critical and external ACH delays are not acceptable.

## Step-by-Step Guide

**Step 1 — Open the Transfer Form**

Navigate to **Move Money > Transfer** or click the Transfer shortcut from the Dashboard Quick Pay widget. The transfer form loads with From and To account selectors populated with all eligible accounts linked to the member's profile.

<figure><img src="/.gitbook/assets/accounts-overview-group.png" alt="" width="480"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 2 — Select Accounts, Enter Amount, and Submit**

Select the source account from the **From** dropdown and the destination account from the **To** dropdown, then enter the transfer amount. Click **Transfer Now** to execute immediately — funds are moved in real time and both account balances update on the confirmation screen.

<figure><img src="/.gitbook/assets/account-transfer.png" alt="" width="480"><figcaption></figcaption></figure>

**Step 3 — Review Confirmation**

The confirmation screen displays the transfer amount, the debit and credit accounts, and the effective date. Members can return to the Dashboard to verify the updated balances or navigate to Account Details to confirm the transaction has posted to both accounts.
